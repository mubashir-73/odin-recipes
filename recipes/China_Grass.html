China Grass

China Grass or agar-agar, is a jelly-like substance consisting of polysaccharides obtained from the cell walls of some species of red algae, primarily from "ogonori" (Gracilaria) and "tengusa" (Gelidiaceae). As found in nature, agar is a mixture of two components, the linear polysaccharide agarose and a heterogeneous mixture of smaller molecules called agaropectin. It forms the supporting structure in the cell walls of certain species of algae and is released on boiling. These algae are known as agarophytes, belonging to the Rhodophyta (red algae) phylum. The processing of food-grade agar removes the agaropectin, and the commercial product is essentially pure agarose.

China grass or agar agar is a plant derived gelatin, which is used in making puddings, jellies and custards. Agar agar is commonly known as China grass in India. China grass is a glossy pudding which is made by mixing sweetened milk with China grass powder. The pudding offers a range of benefits that make it a popular choice for dessert lovers. Firstly, it is a healthier alternative to traditional gelatin-based puddings as it is a natural product derived from seaweed. It is free from animal by-products, making it suitable for vegetarians and vegans.

Agar has been used as an ingredient in desserts throughout Asia and also as a solid substrate to contain culture media for microbiological work. Agar can be used as a laxative; an appetite suppressant; a vegan substitute for gelatin; a thickener for soups; in fruit preserves, ice cream, and other desserts; as a clarifying agent in brewing; and for sizing paper and fabrics.

Ingredients

  • China Grass
  • Sugar
  • Milk

Steps

  1. Cut the china grass into small pieces.
  2. Leave them in water for a short while before draining the water immediately.
  3. Put a glass of water on the china grass and stir it continously while heating it.
  4. Once the mixture becomes somewhat dense add milk to it and keep stirring.
  5. Once you see that all the solid chunks have dissolved into solution of milk and water then proceed to pour it into a suitable container based on the shape you want it to be.
  6. Leave for some time,Once it has solidified into the desired shape according to the container you can refrigerate it or eat it as it is.
